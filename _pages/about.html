I specialize in the research fields of computer vision, machine learning, and image processing. I am a former research fellow at the Australian National University working with Prof. Stephen Gould and Prof. Richard Hartley on projects related to deep declarative networks and probability estimation using diffusion models. I received my PhD in computer vision and machine learning, focusing on Markov random field optimization, semantic segmentation, stereo vision, and 3D neural network pruning under the supervision of Prof. Richard Hartley and Dr. Thalaiyasingam Ajanthan at the Australian National University and Data61, CSIRO in 2022. Prior to my doctoral studies, I completed my Master's of Engineering with a specialization in signal and image processing under the supervision of Prof. Lei Zhang and Prof. Mengdao Xing at Xidian University in 2015 and my Bachelor of Engineering exploring the topic of watermark for color digital images under the supervision of Prof. Gui Feng at Huaqiao University in 2012. Before my doctoral journey, I was an engineer working at SenseTime, China from 2016 to 2017 and at ZTE, China from 2015 to 2016.
